The heart of Pakistan

Lahore

Minar-E-Pakistan

Its a national monument.It was built in 1968 to commemorate the Lahore Resolution of 1940, which called for the creation of a separate homeland for Muslims in South Asia.

Lahore Fort

The Lahore Fort, also known as the Shahi Qila, is a citadal located in lahore. It was built in 11th century by the Ghaznavid Empire. It is home to a number of monuments, including the Sheesh Mahal and the Alamgiri Gate.

Badshahi Mosque

The Badshahi Mosque is a Mughal-era mosque. It was built in 1673 by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb and is one of the largest mosque in the world. It is known for its intricate architecture and beautiful marble inlay work.

Bagh-E-Jinnah

Bagh-e-Jinnah, also known as Lawrence Garden, is a historic park. It was established in 1864 by the British Raj and named after Sir John Lawrence, the Viceroy of India at the time.

Lahore Museum

Lahore Museum was established in 1894 and is one of the oldest museum in South Asia. The museum houses a collection of artifacts from the Indus Valley Civilization, The Mughal Empire, and the British Raj.

Lahore Zoo

This zoological garden was established in 1872 and is one of the oldest zoos in South Asia. The zoo houses a variety of animals. The zoo also houses a number of educational programs, including animal shows, educational talks and animal encounters.

Tomb of Jahangir

It was built in 1637 by the Mughal Emperor Jahangir and is one of the most iconic monuments in the city. The mausoleum is a popular tourist destination and is often used as a backdrop for national events.
